Robotics

I am fascinated by the integration of the virtual and the tangible -- the future of engineering. I am heavily involved in Midwestern Regional Design Competition hosted under iRobotics here at UIUC. We design and build a robot in scratch to compete against other universities. Robotics has the potential to revoluntionize safety and the day-to-day life of third world countries, creating a paradigm shift unlike any other in the 21st century.

Computer Science

Computer Science is integral to every aspect of modern engineering, whether its sequencing genomes or predicting the stock market. I look forward to applying CS skills and principles in the robotics and automation realm to usher in the future of driving. I plan on taking AI and ML courses to further my knowledge in this career area.

Android Development

I started Android development back in high school and it quickly turned into a hobby. I started out in MIT app inventor, creating a simple ball bounce game, but since then, I have taught myself more Android syntax and methedologies. I've developed an application that control a device via bluetooth and also an app that implements mood recognition to generate a relevant playlist.

My Projects

A degree is a mere tool, or a gateway, to do something extraordinary in your career. A degree from UIUC Engineering is no exception. FaceTunes is one example of applied learning from the Computer Science curriculum. This application incorporates facial recognition through Microsoft Azure to determine someone's mood and generate a respective playlist. Another one of my projects, the Wireless Light Switch, integrates an Arduino with Bluetooth and an innovate 3D-printed design to allow for wireless control of any light switch from the outside. This devices sticks onto the switch plate and physically flips the switch.
